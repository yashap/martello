The language program begins in Jr. Kindergarten level with a phonics program. The method is very tactile to help the child learn the shape and sound of the letters before they begin putting letters together and reading short vowel words so that by Sr. Kindergarten they are reading long vowels. Whatever grade a child begins at, their level is assessed in order to individualize their program so that strengths can be supported and weak areas developed. By grade 1 students are generally reading and printing from the board and the basics of grammar are introduced be- ginning with action verbs and how to make a sentence from a verb. This leads to printing and composing sentences, followed by the introduction of nouns and composing sentences with both nouns and verbs. In grade 3 we delve into adjectives, adverbs, conjunctions and how the parts of speech work together.

From grade 1 onward we also focus on spelling; children work within their basic vocabulary around a theme, such as Thanksgiving, and are given 10 words drawn from that them for each week’s lesson Grade 2 takes that a step further into memorization and begins an introduction to composition where students are required to write a story on a particular theme which is then edited by a teacher prior to a final corrected copy being produced by the student.

In grades 2 and 3 students do simple novel studies reading in a group with a teacher which helps the children become comfortable speaking within a group. By the time a student enters the 4/5/6/ level they are working on more complex novels, intricate grammar rules along with oral and written comprehension. Additionally throughout the grade structure students are required to choose a topic and do a presentation on that topic, with the level of difficulty increasing in the higher grades.
