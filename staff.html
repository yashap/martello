Staff Bios


Catherine Mills

M. Catherine Mills, Principal Catherine Mills is Martello School. Since she founded the school 27 years ago, she has been a steading guiding force ensuring her vision of a child-centered individualized education was maintained. Catherine has been teaching for 40 years and has taught all grade levels and subjects but has a particular love of the sciences that she eagerly shares with students. Currently Catherine teaches manuscript printing to grade 1 as well as novel study and individualized language studies with a focus on grammar. The rest of her time is devoted to management of the school and development of new programs such as the popular March Break and summer camps.

“I love learning and sharing that love with students. Children are so open to the wonders around them, it is very humbling to be able to introduce them to new concepts and watch them master these and grow. Starting Martello has been the most rewarding experience of my life; our students, teachers and families are truly close and united in providing the best possible experience for each child at Martello”

Catherine has an Associate in Education Degree from the Nova Scotia Teachers` College with an emphasis on teaching early childhood language development. Anne-Lorraine Molloy B.A..Mus,M.Ed.,Vice Principal Anne-Lorraine is invaluable as the other half of the administrative team at Martello. She has been with the Martello family for 20 years, teaches our grade 1 novel study group and directs our music program which runs through all grade levels JK – 6.


Anne-Lorraine Molloy

Anne-Lorraine Molloy B.A..Mus,M.Ed.,Vice Principal Anne-Lorraine is invaluable as the other half of the administrative team at Martello. She has been with the Martello family for 20 years, teaches our grade 1 novel study group and directs our music program which runs through all grade levels JK – 6.

“I love watching young faces and seeing their excitement as they learn new things and accomplish goals. Martello school is a wonderfully supportive with everyone working together to form a caring and exciting learning unit, encouraging academic success and highlighting personal strengths.”

Elaine Foreman

Elaine Foreman, Grades 3-4 Elaine has been teaching for 28 years, happily 15 of those have been here at Martello where she is very popular with our grade 3-4 set. Her experience includes kindergarten, grades 3-5, junior special education as well as adult ESL and literacy classes.

“It’s a privilege to share in the learning process, particularly with children who are so energetic and eager to try new activities and learn new skills. As part of the Martello family I get to teach within a caring school/family atmosphere in a small class sizes allowing me to provide creative, individualized teaching and learning for my students.”

Elaine graduated with honors Bachelor of Science (BSc) from the University of Toronto; took her teacher training at the Faculty of Education, University of Toronto (FEUT); followed by a Masters of Education - Special Education (MEd) from the Ontario Institute for Studies in Education (OISE)


Jody Kish

Jody Kish, Sr. Kindergarten Jody is a wonder as our Kindergarten teacher and has been inspiring this inquisitive group for 22 years.

“I love the rewards of teaching young children and I believe in the Martello program. Martello is a special school because it is like a big family. Carly McDougall

Carly McDougall, Physical Education & Health Carly is the newest member of the Martello family and will be helping our students on the road to a healthy fit lifestyle as our Physical Education & Health teacher for grades K – 6. Carly enjoys working with children and has a flare for dance and drama which she brings into many of her lessons.

“I am a huge advocate for healthy active living, and love bringing creativity into every lesson so students learn the Physical Education and Heath curriculum in a safe, up-beat, and enthusiastic atmosphere. I am very passionate about teaching because it gives me the opportunity to learn something new every day. I am honoured and excited to be the newest member of the Martello staff, and look forward to becoming part of a talented and nurturing educational team.“

Carly received her Bachelor of Honours Science, with a Major in Human Kinetics from the University of Guelph in 2011. Her studies in covered the fields of Physical Education, Biomechanics, Nutrition, and Human Physiology. Carly went on to obtain her Bachelor of Education, specializing in the Primary/Junior divisions, at the University of Western Ontario.


Laura Meilenner

Laura Meilenner, Jr. Kindergarten Laura taught supply in Oakville before she joined Martello in 2013 as our Jr. Kindergarten teacher where she is the centre of the day for this active and inquisitive group.

“My favorite part about teaching is seeing the child grow from learning how to hold a pencil, to reading and solving math problems. It is very rewarding when the child has their “I get it” moment. Being a part of the Martello Family allows full communication with parents and teachers. Parents and teachers have a lot of respect for each other.”

Laura studied Concurrent Education at Trent University with an honors degree in History and then attended Queens University for her final year where she completed her Bachelor of Education Degree.


Laura Sullivan

Laura Sullivan, Grades 5-6 Laura has been a star in the grade 5-6 classroom since she began her teaching career with Martello two years ago. She has taught grades JK through 6 in addition to Health and Physical Education classes.

“I love that as a teacher I learn something new every day that I spend in a school community. Working alongside my students to investigate, discover and create! Building a relationship with each student and watching them grow into a confident and unique individual is my favourite part about the teaching profession. Starting my teaching career at Martello School has been such a blessing! The supportive community of staff, students and families has made my transition into the classroom very enjoyable! Martello sets a high standard for both academics and behavior for students and staff which helps build the kind of tight-knit learning community that truly helps students succeed.”

Laura completed her B.A.H/B.Ed through the Concurrent Education Program at Queen’s University and is an OCT certified K-10 teacher.
